My Projects

IOT BASED ACCIDENT DETECTION AND EMERGENCY SOS DEVICE

An accident is an unpredicted and unintentional event. The vehicle accident especially two-wheeler accident could be a major drawback in several countries, notably in India. This is due to riders' poor behaviours like drunk driving, speed driving etc., which leads to the high risk of people's life. Therefore, motorbike makers have developed safety devices to protect riders from accidental injuries like IoT based tracking devices and panic buttons. This idea proposes an IoT based accident detection and SOS device. This system helps in detecting the accident and sends a message with location to the family and relatives.

EARLY DETECTION AND ALERTING DEVICE FOR PREVENTING TOPPLING OF TRUCKS USING VEHICLE TO VEHICLE COMMUNICATION INFRASTRUCTURE

The main aim of this idea is to prevent toppling of the trucks, so that we use CAN protocol. Controller Area Network (CAN) is a predominant way of interaction between several electronic control units in a vehicle developed by Robert Bosch Gmbh Germany. Our solution to the toppling problem can be implemented by CAN for better communication. Since vehicle possess both low priority and high priority interrupts which are timely responded by the controller. Therefore, the CAN based solution is preferred among other wire communication protocols.

GPS ENABLED BUS TRACKING AND VACANT SEATS MANAGEMENT SYSTEM TO ASSIST PASSENGERS

In this busy world, waiting for the desired bus without knowing actual arrival/departure time is a terrible play. To overcome this problem, an IOT enabled ticket dispenser system integrated with GPS module is used to track the bus for knowing the current location. By using arithmetic operators, the passengers count will be calculated so that the availability of vacant seats and number passengers count will be updated in cloud. In Contemplation with aforementioned problems, this system can give us the exact arrival time of the buses at your station, the number of vacant seats in the arriving bus, the number of future vacancies that will be created from the current station to next station.
